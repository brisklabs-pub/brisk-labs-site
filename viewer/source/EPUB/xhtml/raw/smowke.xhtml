Lesson 2 Affixes in English 

Definition:
An affix is a word part added to a base or root word to change its meaning. Affixes can be either at the beginning of a word (prefix) or at the end of a word (suffix).

Types of Affixes

  1. Prefixes
    Prefixes are added to the beginning of a word to change its meaning.

    Examples:

    • un-: means "not"
      • unhappy (not happy)
    • re-: means "again"
      • rewrite (to write again)
    • dis-: means "opposite of" or "not"
      • dislike (not like)

  2. Suffixes
    Suffixes are added to the end of a word to change its meaning, often altering the wordâ€™s grammatical form.

    Examples:

    • -ful: means "full of"
      • hopeful (full of hope)
    • -less: means "without"
      • careless (without care)
    • -ness: changes an adjective to a noun

 

Practice Example

  • Identify the prefix and base word in: disappear

    • Prefix: dis- (not)
    • Base word: appear
    • Meaning: to not be visible

  • Identify the suffix and base word in: kindness

    • Suffix: -ness (quality of)
    • Base word: kind
    • Meaning: the quality of being kind

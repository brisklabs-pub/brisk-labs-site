Lesson 4. Word Collocations 

Collocations are common combinations of words that frequently appear together in English. Learning these can help students sound more natural and fluent.

Collocations with "Give"

  1. Give advice

    • Example: "The teacher always gives helpful advice."

  2. Give permission

    • Example: "You need to give permission for the trip."

  3. Give a speech

    • Example: "She gave a speech at the graduation ceremony."

  4. Give a hand

    • Example: "Could you give me a hand with this project?"

  5. Give a try

    • Example: "I’m not sure if I’ll like it, but I’ll give it a try."

  6. Give a call

    • Example: "Don’t forget to give me a call when you arrive."

Collocations with "Take"

  1. Take a break

    • Example: "You’ve been working hard; take a break."

  2. Take responsibility

    • Example: "It’s important to take responsibility for your actions."

  3. Take a chance

    • Example: "Sometimes you have to take a chance to succeed."

  4. Take notes

    • Example: "Remember to take notes during the lecture."

  5. Take action

    • Example: "We need to take action to solve the problem."

  6. Take place

    • Example: "The meeting will take place tomorrow."

Understanding collocations helps students use words in ways that sound more natural and native. For instance, instead of saying "make a call," they’ll know to use "give a call," which sounds more fluent. 

Fill in the blank with the correct collocation using "give" or "take."

  1. It’s getting late; you should ___ a break.

    • a) give
    • b) take

  2. She forgot to ___ a call to her mom yesterday.

    • a) take
    • b) give

  3. After a long day, he likes to ___ a shower to relax.

    • a) give
    • b) take

  4. The teacher will ___ you some advice on how to study.

    • a) take
    • b) give

  5. Don’t be afraid to ___ a chance on something new.

    • a) give
    • b) take

  6. Please ___ notes while I explain the topic.

    • a) give
    • b) take

 

Answer Key

  1. b) take – "take a break"
  2. b) give – "give a call"
  3. b) take – "take a shower"
  4. b) give – "give advice"
  5. b) take – "take a chance"
  6. b) take – "take notes"
